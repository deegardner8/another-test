PUERTO MORELOS

It is the northernmost town in the so-called Riviera Maya. It is located 16 km south of the Cancun International Airport and 32 kilometers south of the city of Cancun. Like many other coastal towns and cities, Puerto Morelos was born as the center of activity for local fishermen. Without detaching itself from its origins, today it is positioned among the classic destinations of the Riviera Maya as a more than interesting option.Unlike Cancun and Playa del Carmen, here you can enjoy a relaxed atmosphere and a lot of tranquility in the middle of beautiful beaches that guarantee spend unforgettable days by the sea. The coastal population has a varied offer of hotels and restaurants with cheap prices and low density. To the north and south of the town there are just over five thousand rooms in grand resort type hotels. Off the coast of Puerto Morelos is the Arrecife de Puerto Morelos National Park, a protected natural area whose shallow barrier reef is located a very short distance from the beaches. Due to the low waves, the place is ideal for windsurfing, snorkeling and diving.

SAN MIGUEL

The oldest known vestiges of human occupation on the Island date from the early years of the Christian Era and are attributed to occasional settlements by nomadic groups from the Caribbean. The colonizing group of Cozumel made the Island an important commercial point and gave it its cultural stamp, highlighting two points: the implantation of the cult of Ixchel, mother goddess, patron of weaving, fertility, pregnancy and childbirth, identified with the Moon, making the Island its main sanctuary and the assignment of the toponym that although deformed still identifies the Island. Originally the Mayan name was Cuzaam luumil, by apocope it became Cuzamil and by phonetic deformation, the Hispanics transformed it into Cozumel that we currently use. Etymologically the toponymic can be divided as follows: Cuzam: swallow; Luum: land or place and IL: of belonging to. Literally it can therefore be translated as place or land of swallows.

PLAYA DEL CARMEN

In pre-Hispanic times Playa del Carmen was called Xaman Há (in Mayan language: Xaman Ha ', ‘Agua del Norte’)? and it was a starting point for the Mayans on their pilgrimage to the sanctuary of Ixchel in Cozumel. The first modern settlement dates from the early twentieth century, when a community of fishermen, coconut and gum tree producers settled there. [Citation needed] During the 16th century the Spanish invaded and conquered most of the Mayan sites. However, unlike many others, Playa del Carmen never became a Spanish settlement. [Citation needed] With the arrival of the Spanish in Mexico, it is recorded that Francisco de Montejo was the one who conquered this region in 1526, founding the town from Salamanca where Xel-Ha is currently located. Also, a year later, a Montejo lieutenant, Alonso Dávila, established his camp in Xaman-Ha, which he described as a fishing village. Despite these events, this area remained very sparsely inhabited throughout the colonial period. The founding of Playa del Carmen was due to a chicle company on the eastern coast of Yucatán, which in 1908 obtained from the government of Porfirio Díaz the concession to explore forest resources.
